# LitRPG Good-Enough Bar

Date: 2026-07-21

## Question

What must a ten-chapter Infinite LitRPG story prove before human demo review?

## Reference Facts

- _Defiance of the Fall_ makes the System, classes, skills, survival choices, and protecting family part of the same conflict. Source: [Aethon Books](https://aethonbooks.com/book/defiance-of-the-fall/).
- _Dungeon Crawler Carl_ combines explicit game rules and escalating floors with a central Carl–Donut relationship, allies, betrayal, and problem-solving. Source: [Penguin Random House](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/series/43C/dungeon-crawler-carl/).
- _Azarinth Healer_ foregrounds action, exploration, class growth, and distinct serialized chapter events instead of treating numbers as the story. Source: [Royal Road](https://www.royalroad.com/fiction/16946/azarinth-healer).
- _He Who Fights with Monsters_ couples progression mechanics with a strong protagonist voice, social conflict, and relationships. Source: [Aethon Books](https://aethonbooks.com/book/he-who-fights-with-monsters/).
- _The Primal Hunter_ ties choices, danger, skills, and escalating power to a continuing survival arc. Source: [Aethon Books](https://aethonbooks.com/book/the-primal-hunter/).
- _The Wandering Inn_ makes classes and skills respond to actions, desires, roles, and changes in identity. Mechanics therefore express character rather than sit beside character. Source: [The Wandering Inn Wiki](https://wiki.wanderinginn.com/Classes), a fan-maintained reference linked to chapter evidence.
- OpenAI documents GPT-5.6 Sol as the complex-work tier, Terra as the balanced tier, and Luna as the high-volume tier. All three expose a provider maximum output of 128,000 tokens and a 1.05M-token context window. `max_output_tokens` is optional in the Responses API. Sources: [model selection](https://developers.openai.com/api/docs/guides/latest-model), [Sol](https://developers.openai.com/api/docs/models/gpt-5.6-sol), [Terra](https://developers.openai.com/api/docs/models/gpt-5.6-terra), [Luna](https://developers.openai.com/api/docs/models/gpt-5.6-luna), [Responses create](https://developers.openai.com/api/reference/resources/responses/methods/create).

## Inference

- Popular examples vary in tone, setting, combat density, and pacing. Shared lesson is not imitation. Mechanics create decisions and costs; relationships carry consequences; chapters change state; voice and scene shape vary.
- A System notice alone is not progression. It must alter a choice, capability, risk, objective, or relationship.
- Signal counts catch obvious repetition but cannot prove interest. Human reading remains release authority.
- Arbitrary prose length and spend ceilings caused incomplete scenes and blocked valid retries. They do not measure quality.

## Fixed Pass Bar

Every story passes independently. Six-story averages cannot hide one weak story.

### Canon and completion

- Exactly 10 contiguous chapters, 10 atomic canonical commits, exact trace replay, current source provenance, and zero POV or canon break.
- Every chapter is a complete scene with immediate goal, resistance, meaningful choice, consequence, and forward hook.
- Chapter 10 differs from chapter 1 in at least three areas: situation or location, relationship, capability or resource, knowledge, objective, threat.

### LitRPG System

- Consequential System presence in at least 4 chapters.
- At least 2 decisions use a quest, stat, skill, class, resource, reward, or risk as a real tradeoff.
- Mechanics never replace scene action, dialogue, or character reaction.

### Character and dialogue

- Clear want and flaw under pressure.
- Real dialogue in at least 6 chapters, with no more than 2 dialogue-free chapters in sequence.
- At least 3 exchanges change a plan, relationship, or conflict.
- At least 4 internal or relational turns; at least 2 alter later behavior; at least 3 development categories appear.

### Progression and pacing

- Every chapter changes situation, knowledge, relationship, capability, resource, location, objective, or threat.
- No action or progression signature repeats three times in sequence.
- Chapters 1–3 establish System pressure, want, flaw, and commitment.
- Chapters 4–7 escalate cost, relationship friction, and reversal.
- Chapters 8–10 converge prior choices, pay off a setup, materially change state, and create a stronger next problem.

### Novelty and prose

- At least 8 unique titles; no adjacent duplicate or repeated title template.
- At least 7 distinct four-word openings; no first word opens more than 3 chapters.
- No looping rhetoric, copied paragraph, filler scene, repeated construction, grammar break, or abrupt cutoff.
- No word-count target. Scene completeness and causal movement are the gate.

### Human verdict

- Reviewer can name 3 memorable scenes, 2 relationship turns, 2 System tradeoffs, and 1 earned payoff.
- No more than 1 chapter feels like filler.
- Reviewer would voluntarily read chapter 11.
- Any `revise` verdict blocks that story and the six-story packet.

## Done Rule

One run has a fixed 60-chapter horizon. Provider retries remain finite. Generation stops when all six stories meet the deterministic bar and have recorded human verdicts, or when a concrete provider or external blocker has repeatable evidence. Cost, local output-token, prompt-byte, and prose-length ceilings never stop the run.

## Unknowns

- Ten chapters can prove early progression, not a full 100- or 350-chapter arc.
- Human interest cannot be reduced to one deterministic score. Review evidence must remain visible and editable.

## Decision Impact

Use Sol medium for frame and narration, Terra low for audit, and Luna for bounded background intent. Give frame, narrator, and audit all prior prose. Give each background agent every prior chapter only through its POV-safe canon history. Run six stories to ten chapters, then review against this fixed bar once.
