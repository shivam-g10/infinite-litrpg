# Narrative Review Rubric

Score 0 to 2. Cite text evidence. Any zero in continuity, POV safety, LitRPG mechanics, or arc progress blocks release.

| Dimension          | 0                        | 1               | 2                               |
| ------------------ | ------------------------ | --------------- | ------------------------------- |
| Choice fulfillment | Ignored                  | Weak effect     | Clear causal effect             |
| Character autonomy | Background actors inert  | Generic motion  | Distinct goal-driven motion     |
| POV safety         | Hidden fact leaked       | Ambiguous       | Knowledge boundary clean        |
| LitRPG mechanics   | Contradiction            | Thin or unclear | Consistent and meaningful       |
| Continuity         | Canon break              | Minor strain    | State and history preserved     |
| Arc progress       | Stalled or rushed        | Partial         | Milestone pressure fits clock   |
| Prose              | Repetitive or incoherent | Readable        | Strong voice and scene movement |

Human release owner makes final call. Reviewer agents provide evidence only.

## Ten-chapter deterministic floor

These gates reject obvious failure modes before human review. They do not score interest.

- System consequence: at least 4 chapters pair an explicit quest, class, skill, stat, XP, or resource mechanic with a concrete consequence. Repeating `System` or a status notice does not count.
- System tradeoff: at least 2 chapters pair an explicit mechanic with both a decision and a cost, risk, sacrifice, or foregone option.
- Consequential dialogue: at least 3 chapters contain a substantial spoken directive or commitment followed by evidence that the plan, relationship, or conflict changed. Existing dialogue distribution and density gates still apply.
- Early phase, chapters 1–3: evidence covers setup and commitment.
- Middle phase, chapters 4–7: evidence covers escalation, cost, and reversal.
- Late phase, chapters 8–10: evidence covers convergence, payoff, and a forward hook.
- Ending movement: chapter 10 differs from chapter 1 in at least 3 available dimensions among situation, location, relationship, capability or resource, knowledge, objective, and threat. Canonical progression markers supply state evidence; prose claims alone do not.

Each metric records matching chapter numbers or signal evidence. Reviewers must inspect that evidence. Heuristics can reject shallow structure. They cannot prove causality, voice, memorable scenes, or desire to read chapter 11.

## Human good-enough verdict

The reviewer must cite 3 memorable scenes, 2 relationship turns, 2 System tradeoffs, and 1 earned payoff; mark no more than 1 chapter as filler; and state that they would voluntarily read chapter 11. Any `revise` verdict blocks that story.
